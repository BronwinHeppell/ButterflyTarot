The African Monarch
Did you know?
The African Monarch is believed to be the first documented butterfly in history.
The Pearl Emperor
Did you know?
The Pearl Emperor is diurnal, which means they are only active during daytime, they are also extremely fast flyers.
Boisduvals False Acrae
Did you know?
This butterfly exhibits a remarkable form of mimicry known as "Batesion mimicry"
The Scotch Argus
Did you know?
The Scotch Argus has intricate wing patters that provide excellent camouflage.
The Green hairstreak
Did you know?
During the caterpillar stage, they produce honeydew, which attracts ants. The ants then provide protection from predators and parasites.
The Gatekeeper
Did you know?
Male Gatekeeper butterflies are territorial and defend their territories against other males. They perch on vegetation to wait for females or to challenge intruders.
Prickly Ash Swallowtail
Did you know?
The Prickly Ash Swallowtail feeds on Prickly Ash trees which becomes more toxic when exposed to sunlight, causes dermatitis in humans.
Yellow Angled-Sulphur
Did you know?
These butterflies have a Rapid zigzagging flight pattern, Female Yellow Angled-Sulphurs can mimic the Clouded Sulphur with her wings.
The Giant Swallowtail
Did you know?
This caterpillar has a unique form of mimicry "bird dropping mimicry" it resembles a bird dropping, making it less appealing to predators.
Random Fact
Did you know?
Butterflies have taste receptors on their feet. They use their feet to "taste" the plants they land on.
Random Fact
Did you know?
Butterflies gather at mud puddles to "puddle." They extract essential nutrients and minerals from the damp soil to supplement their diet.
Random Fact
Did you know?
Butterflies have delicate and colorful wings covered in tiny scales. These scales give them their unique patterns and colors.
